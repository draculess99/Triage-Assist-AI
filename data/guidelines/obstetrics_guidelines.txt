**EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT OBSTETRICS GUIDELINES**

**1. Vaginal Bleeding in Pregnancy**
Any pregnant patient presenting with heavy vaginal bleeding (soaking >1 pad per hour) requires immediate evaluation (ESI-2). Obtain large-bore IV access and prepare for potential hemorrhage protocols. If the patient is >20 weeks gestation, they should be evaluated in Labor and Delivery if stable, but initial stabilization happens in the ED.

**2. Preeclampsia / Eclampsia**
A pregnant patient (>20 weeks gestation) or up to 6 weeks postpartum presenting with a systolic BP > 160 or diastolic BP > 110, severe headache, visual changes, or right upper quadrant abdominal pain must be triaged as ESI-2. If seizures occur (Eclampsia), triage as ESI-1 and administer IV Magnesium Sulfate immediately.

**3. Ectopic Pregnancy Risk**
Any female of childbearing age presenting with unilateral lower abdominal pain and vaginal bleeding must have a pregnancy test (hCG). If pregnant, suspect an ectopic pregnancy until a transvaginal ultrasound confirms an intrauterine pregnancy. Triage as ESI-2 or ESI-3 depending on vital signs and pain severity.
